The AI PM Gita Book · in progress
Timeless wisdom · Modern products

The Mahabharata isn't just a story of war — it's a manual for choices. The AI PM Gita translates 18 parvas into 18 lessons for building AI products that create impact, earn trust, and endure — each one tested against real production failures.

The 3 Sins of an AI PM Parva 4

Duryodhana was offered peace three times and refused three times — each refusal a distinct sin: hype without depth, speed without safety, and features without purpose. Every failed AI product walks through at least one of these gates.

"Failed products are rarely built in ignorance. They're built by teams who knew — and shipped anyway."
Designing for When AI Gets It Wrong Parva 13

Abhimanyu learned to enter the Chakravyuha but never heard how to exit — a frame for AI features brilliantly designed for entry, with no way out. Introduces the four failure modes and the Three Rings: Signal, Threshold, Exit.

"The model learned to please, not to help. Nobody files a bug report that says 'the AI was too agreeable.' That's what makes sycophancy the most dangerous failure of all."
